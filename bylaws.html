Bylaws of Clan Faleen

Statement of Purpose

Clan Faleen is a group that has dedicated itself to serving the communities of the world of Elanthia, the world of GemStone III. As a second purpose Clan Faleen promotes good role-playing by teaching new adventurers by example.

Membership

Membership in the Clan Faleen will be open to all inhabitants of Elanthia, regardless of religion, race, color, sex, race, profession or educational background as long as they are dedicated to good role-playing.

All potential members will undergo an Interview with the Herald, Magistrate or Mistress/Master of Initiates and at least one Tribune prior to their initiation.

The potential new member should be prepared to answer questions cover a range of topics from the Clans bylaws to how they would react in certain situations.
Any player may have more than one character in the Clan as long as they can do the services required for each character. No player may have more than one character in an Office position.

Dues

Clan Faleen will require no dues for membership. Faleen is basically an extended family and as such won't charge to be a member of it. Each member of Clan Faleen will be encouraged to turn in a minimum of one sack of furs, skins, gems or other items that are sellable at the pawnshop a month the skins will be sold at the furrier and the gems at the gem shop and the proceeds go into the treasury to fund various Clan activities and needs. Any member that turns in their dues sack will receivepoints toward their next promotion. These skins or gems can be turned into theHerald, Magistrate or the Master/Mistress of Finances for credit.

If in the event Clan Faleen ever acquires a house any member that wishes to be part of the house must pay the required dues.

Required Services

Each member of Clan Faleen will be required to provide a minimum of 2 hours a month of Community service in order to get the Clan's name known. These services can be almost anything as long as it is done in the name of Clan Faleen and no personal monetary gain is made. Below is a partial list of possible services. Lockpicking, Imbedding, Healing, Bashing, Skinning, Foraging, Guiding New Adventurers, Answer new adventurers questions and any other service to the community are all acceptable services.

These services can be done in the presence of any Clan officer or member of Rook rank or higher who will report the service to the Mistress/Master of Initiates for full credit or done and reported by yourself without an officer present and reported to the Master/Mistress of Initiates for half credit. Services can also be completed at a Clan sponsored gather for the purpose of these services.

Each member is expected to attempt to make it to 1 of 2 Clan meetings held each month. If you are going to be unable to attend one of the many meetings held each month please contact the Herald.

The Government of Clan Faleen

Clan Faleen is a family led by the Herald and Magistrate. The Herald has final authority and responsibility for the Clan.

In the event that a Herald perishes or retires, he/she has two choices. 1) Promote the Magistrate to the Position of Herald. In which case the former Magistrate will choose his/her new magistrate. Or 2) Select another worthy member from among the officers of the Clan.

Any new additions to the bylaws must be brought before the Tribune Council. All Clan members will also vote on the new proposals and the majority opinion will be added to the council’s votes as one vote.

Any new ideas that don’t have to be written into the bylaws will be brought before the whole clan.

Any Clan member has the right to propose a change to the bylaws to the Tribunes for consideration no matter what their rank.

The officer’s council shall be made up of the Herald, Magistrate, 5 Tribunes, Mistress/Master of Activities, Mistress/Master of Finances, Mistress/Master of Initiates, and any member of High Raven rank.

There will be a court of 5 Tribunes that will preside over any Clan disputes or termination hearings. The Tribunes shall choose one of their number to act as Speaker for the council. The Herald or Magistrate must be present at any hearing but will only vote in the case of a tie. A minimum of 3 Tribunes must be present with the Herald or Magistrate at any hearing.

Ranks within Clan Faleen

Each rank within the Clan has a certain amount of services that must be preformed before the member will be moved to the next Rank.

Each Rank will have a number of points also that must be met in order to advance; about 50 percent of the points are gained through the requirements for each rank.

Attending any Clan meeting or Function - 10 points.

1 hour of Community service - 20 points.

Actively role-playing - 10 points.

Bringing a new worthy Pledge into the Clan - 10 points.

Writing a news story that is used in the Clan Newsletter - 10 points.

Paying your required skins or gems - 10 points for the first sack per month, and 5 for each additional per month.

Other point making activities may be granted as they are witnessed.

When a member reaches their next rank the point totals goes back to zero to start the build up for their next promotion. Clan events requirement can be made by attending any clan event except clan meetings.

Pledge - an adventurer that has applied to Clan Faleen, undergone their interview for membership and is awaiting their initiation. Each pledge will be assigned a Raven or High Raven or officer that will act as a mentor until the time of their Induction. The mentor’s job will be to determine the Pledges role-playing abilities, and that they observe proper etiquette in the lands.

Initiate - once a Pledge has undergone the Initiation ceremony he/she will be promoted to the rank of Initiate. To be promoted from Initiate to Fledgling you must be with the clan for 2 months and completed a background history on yourself for the official records. A point total of 20 is required.

Fledgling - for promotion to Talon a member must show him/herself to be active in serving the community by being observed by a member of Rook rank or above or an officer for a total of 4 hours, the time does not have to be done all at once, and the member that observes the service should contact the Mistress/Master of Initiates with the time completed as it is completed. Also for promotion you must take part in a minimum of 2 Clan scheduled events. A point total of 120 is required.

Talon - Promotion from Talon to Corbie requires that the member take part in a minimum of 2 Clan scheduled role-play encounters. And is observed doing service in the name of the Clan by a member of Rook rank or above or an officer for a total of 6 hours, the time doesn’t have to be served all at once, and the member that observes the service should contact the Mistress/Master of Initiates with the time completed as it is completed. A point total of 200 is required.

Corbie - All members of Corbie rank must serve at least 8 hours of community service under the supervision of a member of Rook rank or above or an officer. Corbies also must attend a minimum of 2 Clan Scheduled events. A point total of 280 is required.

Rook - Rook members are those that have shown themselves to be extremely dedicated but service doesn’t end here. All Rooks are required to complete 4 hours of service under another member of Rook rank or above and are to Observe and supervise a member of Rook rank of below for 4 hours of service. All Rook also must attend a minimum of 3 Clan Scheduled role-play Encounters. A point total of 320 is required.

Raven - Near the top of the ranking ladder Raven’s take on an added responsibility. They are still required to do 4 hours of service and 4 hours of observing service. Each Raven will be assigned at least one Pledge during their time at this rank; it is the Raven’s job to act as mentor to the Pledge until his/her induction. The Raven is to make sure the Pledge understands what role-playing is, and that they observe proper etiquette. Each Raven also needs to attend a minimum of 3 scheduled events before they are promoted to High Raven rank. A point total of 400 is required.

High Raven - The highest non-officer rank in Clan Faleen. High Raven’s should remain active and assist the lower ranked members with their requirements. A High Raven should complete a minimum of 2 hours service a month and 2 hours observing another member as they serve. Each High Raven is expected to attend a minimum of 4 Clan Scheduled events a year. High Raven’s should acquire 50 points a month.

High Raven’s should acquire 50 points a month.

Clan Officers

All new officer’s must be of Talon Rank or above to be nominated for an office. All current officer’s will be given the Rank of Talon unless they achieved a higher rank prior to their appointment to their office. All officer’s must continue through advancement in the ranks as any other member. The Herald and Magistrate are unaffected by this provision since they are the leading members of the clan and are the ones with the most responsibilities.

All offices are for life, or until the officer decides to step down, leave the lands permanently or until the Herald removes said officer.

All initial offices will be appointed by the Herald, after the initial start up any replacements will be voted on by the Officers council with the exception of the Magistrates position which will always be an appointed position.

All officers must be committed and active in Clan Faleen and must make at least 1 of 2 officer meetings to be held each month. All officers need to be prepared to make any necessary reports at these meetings.

All officers serve the Clan as a whole and are in office for the Clan’s benefit, not the Herald’s.

Master/Mistress of Initiates (historian / secretary) - The main duty of this office is to make sure all Initiates write out their background history for the records, the officer will assist in the writing and editing of the history if the Initiate needs it. The Mistress/Master of Initiates is responsible for keeping the records of the promotion points and who is due for promotion. The Officer will also keep records on clan events. The officer may also be asked to act as scribe in meetings. This officer may perform promotion ceremonies and is the only officer other than the Magistrate and Herald to have this power.

Tribune (5 total) - Tribune’s are responsible for making sure all Clan laws are followed. They are responsible for any necessary rewrites to the bylaws and consider ideas brought to them by Clan members. They will settle disputes between Clan members. At least one Tribune shall be present at any Clan ceremony or interview to act as a witness. All tribunes must know the bylaws from front to back in order to complete their job correctly.

Master/Mistress of Finances - Responsible for keeping track of the Clan money. Collect each members furs or gems each month for sell with the profits going to the Clan coffers. Reports any points earned by members through dues to the Mistress/Master of Initiates.

Master/Mistress of Activities (co-secretary) - The Master/Mistress of Activities is responsible for planning and seeing to the implementation of group hunts, Clan Games, and activities that will bring the Clan to the attention of the community like a Community service night, or an event for community participation. Keeps track of members service to community and reports them to the Mistress/Master of Initiates.

Magistrate - The second in command and the heir apparent of Clan Faleen. Responsibilities include all the duties of the Herald except for termination rights.

Herald - The leader of the Clan. Has total authority and responsibility for the Clan.

Termination of Membership

The Herald may terminate the membership of any member that show him/herself to not be active.

The Herald can terminate the membership of any member that he/she feels doesn’t meet the membership requirements. The member in question may request a hearing with the Tribune council to defend his/her actions. All Clan members will be given 3 days notice of any such hearing. At least 3 Tribunes must be present in order for a hearing to be held.

The Herald may terminate the membership of any member that has committed Treason against the Clan if he can prove such to the Tribune Council. All members will be given 3 days notice for any such hearing. At least 3 Tribunes and either the Herald or Magistrate must be present in order for a hearing to be held.

Any member that is terminated may reapply for membership if he/she can prove that the reason that they were terminated has been resolved. The member would start at the rank of Initiate no matter what rank they were when they were terminate and will remain an Initiate for 2 months time.

Removal of a Herald

A Herald in which his people have no confidence is a Liability. The Officer’s council may call for a vote of No-confidence at any time in which they feel the Herald is neglecting his/her duties. If ¾ths of the members vote that they have no confidence in the Herald’s abilities the Herald is removed from office, and the Magistrate steps up to take the Herald’s spot. The removed Herald may stay as a member of the Clan at the rank of Talon if he/she so wishes.

If a Herald chooses to step down from the office for what ever reason he/she may be given the Rank of High Raven as a place of honor if the council approves.

Crimes against the Clan

Treason

The most severe crime that can be committed against the Clan. This includes but is not limited to providing an enemy with Clan secrets, member information, meeting times and locations to be used in attacks against the Clan. The Herald and the Tribune council in a hearing for the accused will decide on punishment for treason.

Purposeful Murder of a Non-Clan member

We believe there is always a non-violent way to solve every problem. If a Clan member is accused and found guilty of murder of any Elanthian citizen, that member shall have his/her rank and any offices held striped from him/her, they will be reduce to the rank of Initiate and have to work their way back up through the ranks.

Murder of a Clan Faleen member or one of it's allies

If the accused has murdered a member of either Clan Faleen or one of it's allies they will stand before the Tribunes and the Herald for trial. If found guilty of the crime the accused will be banished from Clan Faleen and shunned.

Clan Ceremonies

In order to encourage role playing, the Clan will have a number or ceremonies that each member will partake in as the move through the ranks, at each ceremony they will restate their oath they took on become an initiate in Clan Faleen.

Clan News Letter, Mailing lists, and Message Board

The clan will put out a monthly Newsletter covering Clan events and Elanthian events. The articles will be written by the members of the clan who will be rewarded with points toward their promotions.

Clan Faleen will have 3 mailing lists. The first mailing list will be for official business only and all members will automatically be signed up on it. This list will be used for announcements, and Clan Newsletters. The second mailing list will be used as a Chat in order for those that wish to get to know each other better out of character to do so. Only those members that wish to be on this list will be part of it. The third mailing list will be used by Clan Faleen officers only. Improper use of mailing lists will result in disciplinary action by the Clan Faleen officers council. E-mails containing racial slurs, foul language, or otherwise offensive materials will not be tolerated at any time. The Clan will set up and use a message board for use in announcements to the general public, recruitment, and other items of business.

Clan Holidays and Role Playing Encounters

Clan Faleen will have at least one role-playing envent a month, most set up by the Mistress/Master of Activities.

Clan Faleen has 3 permanent events on their calendar that count as role-playing Encounters.

Feast of Remembrance - this feast is held in June as an occasion to remember those that have left the lands through either Retirement or have perished. At the feast the Book of Remembrance will be read. Any one in attendance at the feast may add family members or friends to the list read after the book has been read. The Book of remembrance shall also be posted here. (Link added later)

Feast of Celebration - this feast is held in November of each year where we celebrate all we have been given by the gods we follow.

Benefactor’s Day - on February 18th of each year we celebrate what the Benefactor did for the homeland by acting as a new adventurers benefactor for the day. Every member should find a young adventurer to assist is some way on this day be it taking them hunting, answering question or buying them some needed equipment.

The remaining 9 months will have events of some sort planned by the Mistress/Master of Activities.
