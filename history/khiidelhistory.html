The Elvin empire flourished for thousands of years, keeping rule and tight reign on other races. Till the horde of Undead came, starting the long and enduring undead war. Though the elves had won the war, with the help of other races, they had lost their empire. Their army had nearly been decimated and they lost most of their rein on the lesser races. There were Seven houses of rule: Faendryl, the masters of spiritual and elemental magic and the major house of rule in the circle; Vaalor, house that provided most the warriors and tactitioners; Nalfein; who used their stealth and political intrigue to gain favor among other houses; Ardenai, woodsman and hunters and very close to the sylvans that stayed in their precious forests after the Urdaemon war; Ashrim, sea faring elves and fisherman; Loenthra, adept in craft and poetry, their bards legendary for their talent; Illistim; Scholars and historians. It was because of the Faendyl using ancient and dangerous magic that the elves won that bloody war, but in the aftermath of it they lost most of their favor among the other houses. Inside the house of Faendryl there were many minor sects dedicated to different magic, from summoner to healer, one very unique and unknown sect were the Ta'Ailen, walkers of dreams, They learned to walk mens dreams by use of the arts. Their abilities helped the Faendryl greatly in the game of houses, keeping them high in power. Even the Nalfein's political intrigue couldn't out do what Ta'Ailen seen in a rival houses dreams. The dreamers learned that the other six houses were going to betray the Faendryl and exile them to the cursed land they had blistered with their art. This sect fled before the other houses attacked Faendryl and had them banished vowing to unite the seven houses and restore the elven empire to rain in Elanthia. Many generations they stayed in hiding, passing their vow from father to son, along with their knowledge of dreamwalking and magic, that someday they would reunite the seven houses. Ta'Ailen were very adept in spiritual magic, with that talent they improved themselves and sought out others by means of dreams. Over time their numbers grew but also the Ta'Ailen divided into two groups, one wanting too keep elven blood pure for the reunion, restoring of the old ways and the old rule where elves were the rulers and lesser races their subjects, this sect called themselves the jintar, pure ones. The other however wished to bring new rule to the lands, where all races would be considered equal among the great houses, adopting houses of their own, they were called the Kan'tel, bringers of change.

A few years after went mostly uneventful, usually the Kan'tel wanting to move right then and restore order and the Jintar arguing that they had not learn enough about the other houses to risk showing themselves, even age old betrayal runs strong with elves. Eleida was beginning to grow in favor and in size, she was carrying's Markael's child in her belly.
