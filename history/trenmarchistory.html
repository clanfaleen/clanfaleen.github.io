Trenmarc lived in a small remote Faleen village. His Father was a historian and mother a seamstress/weaver. Trenmarc was in business with his brother, both were trappers/fur traders. Trenmarc was married to Serian, a girl that had been apprenticed to his mother. Life was simple and for the most part uneventful.

Checking his trap lines one evening, Trenmarc witnessed a group of golden armored soldiers mounted on horses and large griffins. As he watched the regiment heading south in a hurry, battered and tired, he couldn’t help but wonder at their purpose in his small lands. He had heard stories of golden warriors known as the Order of the Golden Scale. He had also heard tales of the present fight they had taken on against the Blue One’s armies. Curious as to the reasoning of their presence here, he quickly gathered his spoils for the day and hurried back to tell his loved ones of his wondrous sightings.

Upon nearing his village he soon saw clouds of smoke where there should be a village. Dropping his gear, and fearing the worst, Trenmarc ran as fast as his feet could carry him through the trails. He arrives at the entrance of his village in what seemed to take a lifetime of running. Frantically he tries to find any signs of his friends, family, anyone... only to find that he is the last survivor. He realizes to his dismay that this is from where the army had retreated. The Great Golden Army had been defeated trying to save his village. The grounds are littered with the bodies of mostly orcs and undead creatures of various evil creation. Battling his way through the few straggling beasts that were picking through the village spoils, Trenmarc finally finds the charred remains of what he believes to be his family. He marks a small shrine for his loved ones, lowering each of their bodies back into the arms of the earth that bore them. He spends a great deal of his time taking care to bury the remains of many of the other villagers he finds strewn about. He mournfully cradles a glowing stone that he found clenched in the partially charred hand of his wife Serian. The glowing nature of the stone beckoning to him as if to say that her very soul rest within it yearning for his comforts. He gingerly places the stone in a pocket close to his heart, the only reminder of his innocent love.

After all was taken care of he gathered his meager possessions and headed south, following the trail of the Order of the Golden Scale. Finding the headquarters, he enters their ranks and begins his training. After many moons, the former fur trader becomes an accomplished swordsman. He fights as one of the Order for 3 years before the battle that decided his fate. The Order had fallen back to the Benefactor's Capital, their numbers depleted, they find themselves gravely overmatched. In an effort to preserve their names sake, the leaders decide that 50 of their number would escape while the others would fight to the end. Thus, gods willing, those that escaped would be able to carry on in the hope defending the lands another day. Lots were drawn to decide the fates of each of the faithful warriors. Trenmarc found himself to become one of these favored by the gods. These 50 regretfully retreated, all to well knowing the fate of those whom stood to guard their escape. These 50 came to be known as "Seekers". Their duty, to find the remnants of the Clans that had made it to other towns and prepare them to stand and face evil of the Blue One’s armies once again and reclaim their homelands. As he makes his way out of the battle, to start his desperate journey, Trenmarc is stopped by one of his close brethren. Removing his griffin engraved bracers he hands them to Trenmarc. No words are passed, there is no need. This will be the last time they will see each other, as the golden warrior stands to protect the path that his friend (Trenmarc) must evade too. With a nod and a warm smile, the warrior nudges Trenmarc toward the escape route. Then the smile straightens, his eyes narrow, the bloodied golden armor dented and damaged... he turns to face the army that all to soon is cutting through his falling comrades. Like a bright shining statue of the gods he stands there blocking the path as Trenmarc makes his way, already out of sight. Trenmarc can hear his friends all too familiar war cries, and just when he thinks it is over he still hears his cries over the sounds of the battles, until he is so far away he can no longer hear the battle. Smiling to himself, he thinks," Give them hell for me my friend."

Trenmarc traveled much of the lands. Searching frantically for parts of his past and Order lost. He did eventually finding one such group in Elanthia, where he has settled, and since started his own clan Faleen also.
