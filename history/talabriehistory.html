It's funny how strongly events of the past affect attitudes of the present. I've often been asked why I have such a fear of magic and those who rely on it's power. To answer these questions I must return to the past, long before my birth.

Oh yes, first let me introduce myself. I am Talabrie D'Thrifty, half elven rogue and daughter or the dark elven swordswoman, Talabrina Darckedge, for who I was named and the human rogue Larenfro Thrifty. I was raised in a small village close to the town of Icemule Trace. It was a place of friendship and peace until they came.

They were a group of wizards and sorcerers who were unhappy about the treatment the warriors in the village received. The warriors, being the protectors of the village were given the better homes and the pick of the meat gained in their hunts. The magic users were overlooked in many instances and they weren't pleased with this fact. The battles that were fought were fierce and many lives were lost on both sides. My mother, knowing her skills with a weapon were not sufficient enough to protect herself, hid during a good portion of the fighting. My aunt Talondria was spared because she fought on the side of the casters. When the opportunity presented itself my mother fled in fear of those who wished to destroy her home. She would return a few days later to see the destruction caused by those who had used their power for personal gain. From this point on she swore never to trust any who would use magic for any purpose.

Mother made her way to a place called Wehnimer's Landing and began building a new life for herself. Alone in the world and armed with only sword, shield and her meager skills she did fairly well for herself. She learned the way of the sword quickly and soon gained more confidence in her ability. Then one day she met a handsome rogue while out on one of her many hunts and fell hopelessly in love. There was only one problem. He was human. She made many friends during her time in this town and her love for the rogue grew deeper with each passing day. Then one day The man asked my mother for her hand. She was so shocked she could hardly speak. There was one thing standing in their way. He must first get approval from her cousin, Romero, since He was the last surviving male in her family. My father tried to explain that it didn't matter what her family thought of their union, only that they loved each other and wished to build a life together. She refused to answer him until he honored her request and removed her armor, put away her weapons and shield and knelt down in the very spot they had chosen to hunt that day.

Father was beside himself with the fear of watching these creatures harm the woman he loved and facing her cousin. After Mother was struck the first time he agreed to honor her request and went in search of Romero. After a long conversation and many heated words he agreed to the union and the two were wed the following Thrakmas. Mother was surprised to betaken to such a lovely chapel. Father hadn't told he of the gift he has planned for her that day.

Everything was going better than either had dreamed. Until one warm spring day just a few months after their wedding. Mother went out in search of her beloved husband to give him some wonderful news. They were expecting the first of what she hoped would be many children. She stopped to rest at the Wayside Inn when a close friend found her. They greeted each other with a warm hug when she noticed the sad look on his face. She asked what was troubling him and he just led her to a table and gently sat her down. This worried her more than she cared to admit. He began telling her of a terrible battle that had been fought between her husband and a swarm of leapers. She knew the news he brought her was grim even before he finished the account. Her husband was a weak fighter. His skill was with his lockpicks, not his sword.

Before he finished the tale she was in tears. Her friend comforted her as best he could and stayed with her until she calmed down. She explained to him about the child growing inside her and how she would leave the city and move someplace quiet to raise the baby. He offered to accompany her but she refused the offer. She preferred to be alone. She only asked that he look out for the child if he was ever needed. He agreed. He would do as she asked because he loved her. This would be a fact Mother never knew.

So Mother returned once again to her homeland. She had hoped things would be different than when she left so many years ago. They were different but not for the better. The warriors of the village were treated no better than slaves, to do the heavy work and to lead the patrols that protected the village. Mother was immediately accepted as one of those warriors. She was able to reclaim her family's home though, A luxury not many had been given. The house had been too small for their purposes and had belonged to my aunt after Mother had left.

Once I was born Mother had to keep me hidden due to the fact that my blood wasn't 'pure'. I was part human. This would not have been tolerated by those who overlooked the running of the village now. So all my time was spent indoors or hidden away someplace if anyone stopped by to give Mother work orders. I spent my whole young life locked away and mostly alone. Over the years Mother taught me what she could about how to use a weapon and how to survive in the world on my own. I was very worried about her when she began to get thinner that was normal and I began asking questions. That was when she told me of my father and what a great man he had been. She told me of what this village had been like in her youth and what had been done to make her leave. I had witness what the magic users had called punishment and was afraid. Men were hurt with no marks left on their bodies. They were tortured in ways I could only dream of. Luckily Mother was spared any of these terrors because she followed orders and because my aunt had served them so well. I was told stories of her as well and wondered what had become of her.

Over the years I had discovered my father's belongings and would spend time trying to pick simple locks with his picks. Until the day Mother caught me trying to pick the lock on one of the many chests she had in the house. I wanted to know what was inside. This was the first and only time we ever fought. I had a right to chose my future. Why Was I forbidden to learn my father's trade? Finally she agreed with one condition. That I learn to protect myself as well as pick a lock. This seemed very reasonable to me since I enjoyed the weapon training almost as much as the boxes. So my days were spent learning to protect myself as well as trying to get those darned chests open.

Then one day I succeeded gentling the largest of them open. I was so excited that I was careless. I flung open the box to find a beautifully crafted sword and shield inside. As my flingers wrapped around the hilt of the blade I received the worst jolt of my young life. I was stunned for only a second but it was enough. I looked at my hand and found I was badly burned where My skin had come in contact with the weapon. I had to know what could cause so much pain. then I knew. It was a magical blade. I quickly closed the chest never wanting to open it again.

The next box I got opened held an even bigger surprise. There was a lockpick case nestled into the most wonderful set of ruby studded armor I had ever seem. Why did mother chose not to wear such a thing into the battles she fought for the village? I would have to ask her. This time instead of reaching for the armor though, I reached for the case resting atop it. As I was getting ready to remove it from it's place I was once again greeted with a terrible jolt. My fingers had brushed against the lovely armor it sat upon. More magic. Now I knew why Mother didn't wear it. Over the next few years I learned more about the magical properties of weapons and armor but it seemed Mother's health was failing. I would hear her cry in the night when she thought I was sleeping. She began speaking to someone as she sat in front of our small fire. Eventually she stopped the lessons she had thought were so important in the beginning. This went on for quite some time.

One morning I awoke to find Mother in her bed with the most beautiful expression on her face. She actually looked truly happy. I had never seen her look so good. There had always been shadows of sadness behind her eyes even when she was smiling. There were no shadows to be found. I investigated further and discovered my mother, whom I loved with all my heart, had been my only companion all these years, my dearest friend, was dead. What was I to do now? She had cared for me and kept me hidden for so long. How would I survive without her? Where would I go? then I remembered the stories and decided to seek out the friends she had left behind. She was with my father once again and was happy for the first time in many years. My life was just beginning.

I quickly packed up what I could, being careful with the enchanted items so I wouldn't get burned again and set out for the town she had spoken so fondly of. I looked back in tears because of what had to be left behind. Perhaps one day I would be able to reclaim it all and the village as well. I had such big dreams then.

It was a long, hard journey but I made it alive. I finally made it to the gates of a very large, busy town. People were walking in and out the gates and never even noticed me standing there taking in all the sights and sounds. I stopped someone and asked where I was and he told me I had arrived at the Landings. I thanked him as he went on his way looking back at me in confusion. All of a sudden I felt a large hand rest upon my shoulder. I spun around, sword in hand ready to take on any threat. He took a quick step back with his hands in plain sight showing he had no weapon and blushed slightly. He explained that I kind of reminded him of a friend he had lost long ago and began to walk away.

Fearful of being alone in such a big place I quickly asked who it was I reminded him of. I had to ask him to repeat the name because it sounded a lot like mine. The second time he said it I realized he was speaking of my mother Talabrina. I instantly told him who I was and how the woman he had mistaken me for was my mother. I asked him if he knew any of her friends and he told me he knew them all. He also told me how much he had loved my mother and had wished to go with her on her journey. She had denied his request and he was hurt by the refusal. But had since gone on with his life.

The next few days were a blur of faces and new friends. I met everyone who had known my mother. After the third day it was all getting to be too much and needed some time alone. I headed out to the cliffs close to town to do a little hunting. That was when I met him. I was hacking away at a creature who decided I looked like a tasty lunch when he approached. He asked me if I had any treasure boxes that might need opening and as luck would have it I did.

I found out his name was Zackrim and we hunted the day away together. He was a handsome elven rogue who had stolen my heart with the first words he spoke. If Mother had felt like this when she met my father, no wonder she missed him for all those years. I understood so much now.

The next few years couldn't have been better. I was now allowed to pick boxes, I had made a promise to my 'Uncle' to wait until I had trained a bit more before trying it and for the most part I stuck to the agreement. I knew it was only a matter of days before Zack would ask me to marry him. We were so good together. Always by each other's side and I was a good partner for him because like my father, he was a weak fighter. I was filled with fear each time I found out he went hunting without me. I was so afraid of receiving the same news Mother had received all those years ago. but there was really only one shadow darkening my life.

My 'Uncle' who I loved like a father had met with my Aunt Talondria. The two fought constantly. It was obvious Talon hated the choice Mother had made and never had a good word to say about her sister. Then one day my Uncle had had enough and attacked my aunt. Since she wasn't expecting it she was nearly killed before she had a chance to answer with an attack of her own.

Soon after the confrontation I learned that the two were engaged. This was to be the beginning of the end of my happy life. Just a few weeks after they announced their engagement Word was sent that my uncle had been killed trying to aid someone else. But that was his way. He always put others before himself.

But I still had Zack to get me through these hard times and his strength was a great help to me. Not long after I was sent word that I had lost my grandfather as well. I was losing all those I loved one by one. I held on to Zack like a lifeboat on a stormy sea. 'Please don't let me lose him too', I thought over and over. This was the saddest point in my life and didn't know how much more I could take.

then one day my greatest fears were realized. Just days before we were to announce our engagement I awoke to find a message beside my pillow with a red rose beside it. My dear sweet Zack had been called away and would possibly never return. My beloved rogue was gone too. I had no one left. I was alone again with no hope of ever finding real happiness. I spent days in my room just crying, not eating and refusing to see anyone. I gave up all my lockpicks except those my father had left to me. I even stopped hunting. My mother's sword was coated in a layer of rust in no time. I had no hope of ever seeing daylight again. I spent time alone waiting to die.

I spent the next four years alone, with no one to love and no one to love me. I spent the days walking in a fog searching for a man I was never able to find hoping one day Zack would return to me in some form. Then I net Zenoir. He was a young giantman who seemed more lost that I felt. I took him in and began caring for him. He is a sweet enough boy anyway.

Not long after I got a message from my cousin Mav that said a friend of his was in need of a locksmith and I met Trenmarc. This was the beginning of a new life. I learned of a group of people who wanted to make a difference in the world around them and I was very interested. In the mean time Zenoir just had to introduce me to his father Dantilli and we hit it off right away.

My young son seemed to believe Dan and I should get married and started pushing us together. I'm still not sure if this was good or bad. My heart still belongs to another and I'm sure that will always be the case. I do regret that no matter who I become involved with in the future there will always be a shadow lurking in the background. One of a handsome elven rogue who stole the heart of a young girl so many years ago.

I have since been Pledged and initiated into the Clan Faleen and feel as though I belong to a family for the first time in my entire life. My aunt Talondria is still wandering around town someplace but she and I were and will never be close. Dantilli and I have become engaged but there is a fly in that ointment. I received a message not too many days ago that my beloved Zack is trying to make his way home to me. Perhaps this story will end happily after all.
