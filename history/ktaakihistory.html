Beginnings I grew up on Wisraith Lane just near House of the Rising Phoenix. We children in the neighborhood played hide-and-seek in summer twilight or ran after the mutt, who barked noisily and chased sticks, which he never brought back. It was then I heard my father say so often, "Alive is good!" Being what I am, I more often heard "Ktaaki, get offa me, ye great clumsy cow!" I towered over my playmates almost from the time I could walk and if I was not tripping myself, I was knocking some one or other of them off their feet. Many a playmate went home to have bruises attended while I went home to have hurt feelings soothed. In time I moved on from hide-and-seek and began to take my place in the life of the Landing. There was no question about my life’s work; I’d been destined for Lorminstra’s service even before I was born. Far from feeling fettered by this understanding, I felt the comfort of knowing I would always have a place and a purpose, no matter how clumsy I was. My training is for the service of resurrection and the release of undead souls. A brief sojourn among those of the bathhouse frightened me so much for my soul that I resigned within just a few days. A kindly gentleman pointed out an old warrior to me and told me to listen to his story. No, I don’t remember his name. He wore gloves with the fingers cut out though. I always thought that was odd. Living With My Grandmother for good or ill, one of the forces in my earlier life was my grandmother, Amaryllis called the Firehand, a wizard as you might expect. For 20 years she romped through the Landing, flirting and slaying with the single-mindedness of those who literally have all the time in the world to learn a thing or pursue a hobby to it’s last least detail. She was passionate; she loved without boundaries for as long as such a thing could last; the last warrior that she fell for so precipitately broke her heart and she never went out very much after that. I only knew her after she had closed herself up in her room at the Silvergate Inn and took to her magical studies with the same intensity she had poured into everything else. On one of her rare forays outside the Silvergate, she took me to Kai’s Shrine, west aways of the Kobold Forest. Up the hill we trudged, through a grove of trees, to be met by the splendor of the Shrine itself and the eight stones that guard and embellish it. She suggested with some force that I study the stones and take their messages to heart; she then left me and went into the Shrine to--I was never sure what her purpose was there. Perhaps she prayed, perhaps she cursed, and perhaps she merely went because the statue within reminded her so strongly of her last best lover. That is the reason I always thought was the true one. I took her advice, an easy thing to do once I had read the first talisman. It so struck me and its words were so poetically woven that my eyes spilled over and it was some little time before I could go on to the rest of them. They are written in my heart now, and I knew my own love when I saw him because I knew they were written in his heart too. The most important person in my life now is the splendid warrior and Tribune of Clan Faleen, Mav Granitehand. The most important day of my life will be our joining, and I rejoice every day that he loves me, as I love him.
